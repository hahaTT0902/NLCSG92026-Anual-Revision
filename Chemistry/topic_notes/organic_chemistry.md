# Chemistry Topic Note — Organic Chemistry

## Crude oil
Crude oil is a mixture of hydrocarbons formed from ancient biomass. It is separated into fractions by fractional distillation.

## Fractional distillation
In fractional distillation, hydrocarbons are separated based on boiling point. Smaller molecules with lower boiling points rise higher in the column.

## Alkanes
Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons with single covalent bonds only. They are relatively unreactive and are commonly used as fuels.

## Alkenes
Alkenes are unsaturated hydrocarbons containing at least one C=C double bond. They are more reactive than alkanes and can be tested using bromine water.

## Cracking
Cracking breaks long-chain hydrocarbons into smaller, more useful molecules, often producing alkenes. This helps meet demand for fuels and feedstock chemicals.

## Polymerisation
Addition polymerisation joins many alkene monomers into a long-chain polymer. Polymers have many uses in packaging, clothing and industry.

## Environmental issues
Combustion of fuels can produce carbon dioxide, contributing to climate change. Incomplete combustion can produce carbon monoxide and soot.

## Common exam ideas
- Explain how fractional distillation works.
- Compare alkanes and alkenes.
- Describe cracking and polymerisation.
- Discuss the environmental impact of fuel combustion.
