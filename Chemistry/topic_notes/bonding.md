# Chemistry Topic Note — Bonding

## Atomic structure and electrons
Atoms contain protons, neutrons and electrons. Electrons occupy shells or energy levels. Bonding happens because atoms tend to gain stable outer electron arrangements.

## Ionic bonding
Ionic bonding happens between metals and non-metals. Electrons are transferred from metal atoms to non-metal atoms. This forms positive and negative ions, which are held together by strong electrostatic attraction.

## Covalent bonding
Covalent bonding happens between non-metal atoms. Pairs of electrons are shared so that atoms achieve stable outer shells. Covalent bonds can form molecules or giant covalent structures.

## Metallic bonding
Metallic bonding occurs in metals. Positive metal ions are attracted to a sea of delocalised electrons. This explains properties such as electrical conductivity and malleability.

## Dot and cross diagrams
Dot and cross diagrams are used to show electron transfer or sharing. They help explain how bonding forms.

## Structure and properties
Different types of bonding produce different structures, which lead to different physical properties such as melting point, conductivity and hardness.

## Common exam ideas
- Compare ionic, covalent and metallic bonding.
- Draw dot and cross diagrams.
- Explain why ionic compounds conduct when molten or dissolved.
- Explain why metals conduct electricity.
