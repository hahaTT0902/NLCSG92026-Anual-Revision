# Chemistry Topic Note — Quantitative Chemistry

## Relative atomic mass and relative molecular mass
Relative atomic mass (Ar) compares the mass of an atom to carbon-12. Relative molecular mass (Mr) is the total of the relative atomic masses in a molecule.

## The mole
The mole is a counting unit used in chemistry. One mole contains Avogadro’s number of particles. It allows chemists to measure amounts of substances using mass.

## Moles and mass
The key equation is:

moles = mass / molar mass

This equation allows conversion between grams and moles.

## Balanced equations and ratios
A balanced equation shows the ratio in which substances react. Mole ratios come directly from the coefficients in the equation.

## Reacting mass calculations
To calculate how much product is made:
1. Find moles of the known substance.
2. Use the equation ratio.
3. Convert back to mass if needed.

## Limiting reactant
The limiting reactant is the reactant that gets used up first. It determines the maximum amount of product formed.

## Concentration
For solutions, concentration links the amount of solute to the volume of solution. This is useful in titration and neutralisation questions.

## Common exam ideas
- Calculate Mr from a formula.
- Convert between grams and moles.
- Use mole ratios in balanced equations.
- Identify the limiting reactant.
