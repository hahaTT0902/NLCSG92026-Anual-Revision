# Grade 9 Chemistry — Mind Map Summary

## 1. Test overview
- The end-of-year test checks chemistry knowledge from the start of the academic year.
- It assesses theory and fundamental chemistry skills.
- Questions may combine recall, application and practical reasoning.
- The test may include multiple choice, short response and graph/data questions.

## 2. How to prepare
- Start revision at home on all covered topics.
- Use the revision booklet provided before Easter.
- Learn summaries before practising questions.
- Focus on both content knowledge and calculations.
- BBC Bitesize Science was recommended in the map.

## 3. Quantitative chemistry
### Core calculations
- Calculate relative atomic mass and relative molecular mass.
- Use formulas to find molecular mass.
- Understand the mole as a counting unit.
- Link moles to mass using molar mass.

### Reacting mass calculations
- Use balanced equations to compare substances.
- Convert between mass, moles and formula ratios.
- Identify the reactant used up first.
- Use mole ratios to determine maximum product yield.

### Advanced quantitative skills
- Use concentration relationships involving moles and volume.
- Rearrange equations to find unknown values.
- Use titration data to determine concentrations.
- Apply balanced equations to neutralisation calculations.

## 4. Bonding
### Structure of the atom
- Know protons, neutrons and electrons.
- Understand electron arrangement and shells.
- Atomic structure links to bonding and properties.

### Types of bonding
- Ionic bonding forms between metals and non-metals and involves electron transfer.
- Covalent bonding forms between non-metals and involves electron sharing.
- Metallic bonding occurs in metals and involves positive ions in a sea of delocalised electrons.

### Representing bonding
- Use dot and cross diagrams.
- Show electron transfer or sharing.
- Explain how stable outer shells are formed.

## 5. Organic chemistry
### Crude oil and fractional distillation
- Crude oil is a mixture of hydrocarbons.
- Fractional distillation separates hydrocarbons by boiling point.
- Different fractions have different chain lengths and uses.

### Alkanes
- Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons.
- They are generally less reactive than alkenes.
- Combustion of alkanes can produce carbon dioxide and water, or pollutants if incomplete.

### Alkenes
- Alkenes are unsaturated hydrocarbons with a double bond.
- They are more reactive than alkanes.
- Bromine water is used to test for alkenes.

### Cracking and polymerisation
- Cracking breaks large hydrocarbons into smaller, more useful molecules.
- Alkenes can undergo addition polymerisation.
- Polymers are made of repeating units joined into long chains.
