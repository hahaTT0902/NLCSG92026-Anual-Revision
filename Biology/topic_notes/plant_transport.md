# Biology Topic Note — Plant Transport

## Why plants need transport systems
Plants need water and mineral ions for photosynthesis and cell processes. They also need to move sugars made in leaves to other parts of the plant.

## Xylem
Xylem transports water and mineral ions from the roots to the stem and leaves. Xylem vessels are dead, hollow tubes with thickened walls that help support the plant.

## Phloem
Phloem transports sugars and other dissolved substances around the plant. This movement is called translocation. It can move materials both up and down the plant depending on need.

## Stomata
Stomata are tiny openings in the leaf, mainly on the underside. They allow carbon dioxide to enter and oxygen and water vapour to leave. Guard cells control whether stomata are open or closed.

## Transpiration
Transpiration is the loss of water vapour from leaves. It creates a pull that helps move water up the xylem. Factors such as temperature, wind and humidity affect the rate of transpiration.

## Root hair cells
Root hair cells absorb water and mineral ions from the soil. Their long extension increases surface area, improving absorption.

## Common exam ideas
- Compare xylem and phloem.
- Explain how stomata are linked to gas exchange and water loss.
- Describe how root hair cells are adapted.
- Explain factors affecting transpiration.
