# Biology Topic Note — Circulation and Immunity

## The circulatory system
The circulatory system transports oxygen, nutrients, hormones, heat and waste products around the body. In humans it is a double circulatory system: one circuit goes to the lungs and one goes to the rest of the body.

## The heart
The heart is a muscular pump. The right side pumps deoxygenated blood to the lungs. The left side pumps oxygenated blood to the body. Valves prevent backflow.

## Blood vessels
- **Arteries** carry blood away from the heart under high pressure.
- **Veins** carry blood back to the heart and have valves.
- **Capillaries** are tiny vessels where exchange of substances happens.

## Components of blood
- **Red blood cells** carry oxygen using haemoglobin.
- **White blood cells** defend the body against infection.
- **Platelets** help blood clot.
- **Plasma** carries dissolved substances such as glucose, carbon dioxide, hormones and urea.

## Coronary heart disease
CHD happens when the coronary arteries become narrowed by fatty deposits. This reduces the oxygen supply to the heart muscle and can lead to chest pain or heart attacks.

## Non-specific defence
The body’s first lines of defence include skin, mucus, stomach acid and inflammation. Phagocytes can engulf and digest pathogens.

## Specific immune response
Lymphocytes recognise foreign antigens and produce antibodies. Some become memory cells, allowing a faster response if the same pathogen returns.

## Vaccination
Vaccines contain weakened or inactive forms of pathogens, or their antigens. They stimulate the immune system to produce memory cells without causing the full disease.

## Common exam ideas
- Compare arteries, veins and capillaries.
- Explain how vaccination protects populations.
- Describe the roles of different blood components.
- Explain the impact of coronary heart disease.
