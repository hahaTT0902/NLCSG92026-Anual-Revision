# Biology Topic Note — Cells and Transport

## Cell theory basics
Living organisms are made of cells. Cells are the basic units of structure and function. In multicellular organisms, cells specialise and work together in tissues and organs.

## Animal and plant cells
Animal cells contain a nucleus, cytoplasm, cell membrane, mitochondria and ribosomes. Plant cells contain all of these and also have a cellulose cell wall, chloroplasts and a permanent vacuole.

## Specialised cells
Specialised cells are adapted to perform particular functions. For example, red blood cells carry oxygen, root hair cells absorb water and mineral ions, and nerve cells transmit electrical impulses.

## Diffusion
Diffusion is the net movement of particles from a region of high concentration to a region of low concentration. It happens because particles move randomly. Diffusion is important in gas exchange and absorption.

## Osmosis
Osmosis is the movement of water molecules through a partially permeable membrane from a dilute solution to a more concentrated solution. Osmosis explains water movement into and out of cells.

## Active transport
Active transport is the movement of substances from low concentration to high concentration using energy. It allows root hair cells and the gut to absorb substances efficiently when diffusion alone is not enough.

## Exam focus
- Compare plant and animal cells.
- Distinguish diffusion, osmosis and active transport.
- Explain how structure helps function.
