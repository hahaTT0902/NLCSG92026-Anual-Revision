# Biology Topic Note — Enzymes and Biological Molecules

## Enzymes
Enzymes are biological catalysts made of protein. They speed up reactions by lowering activation energy. Each enzyme has an active site with a specific shape.

## Lock-and-key / specificity
A substrate fits into the enzyme’s active site. Because the active site has a specific shape, enzymes are specific to their substrates.

## Temperature and pH
Temperature and pH affect enzyme activity. Low temperatures slow reactions because particles move less often. High temperatures can denature enzymes by changing the shape of the active site. Extreme pH can also denature enzymes.

## Biological molecules
- Carbohydrates provide energy.
- Proteins are needed for growth, repair and enzymes.
- Lipids store energy and help insulation.

## Food tests
Common food tests include iodine for starch, Benedict’s for reducing sugars, Biuret for protein and emulsion test for lipids.

## Exam focus
- Explain why enzymes are specific.
- Describe how temperature affects enzymes.
- Identify functions of carbohydrates, proteins and lipids.
- Interpret food test results.
