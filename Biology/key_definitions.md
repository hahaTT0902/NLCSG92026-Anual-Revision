# Biology — Key Definitions

- **Cell**: the basic structural and functional unit of life.
- **Diffusion**: net movement of particles from high to low concentration.
- **Osmosis**: movement of water through a partially permeable membrane from a dilute solution to a more concentrated solution.
- **Active transport**: movement of substances against the concentration gradient using energy.
- **Enzyme**: a biological catalyst.
- **Digestion**: breakdown of large insoluble molecules into small soluble molecules.
- **Absorption**: movement of digested food molecules into the blood.
- **Artery**: blood vessel carrying blood away from the heart.
- **Vein**: blood vessel carrying blood back to the heart.
- **Capillary**: tiny blood vessel where exchange occurs.
- **Vaccine**: substance that stimulates immunity against a disease.
- **Xylem**: tissue that transports water and mineral ions.
- **Phloem**: tissue that transports sugars and dissolved food.
