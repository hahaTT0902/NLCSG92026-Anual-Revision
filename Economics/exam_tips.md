# Economics — Exam Tips

1. Define key terms accurately before explaining them.
2. Draw diagrams neatly and label axes and curves.
3. Use arrows to show shifts, not movements, when appropriate.
4. In data response, quote evidence from the extract.
5. Always explain why a change happens, not just what happens.
6. For taxes and subsidies, mention effects on price, quantity and stakeholders.
7. For PED, explain responsiveness, not just formula ideas.
8. Use ceteris paribus correctly.
9. Distinguish positive analysis from value judgement.
10. Keep answers clear and cause-effect focused.
