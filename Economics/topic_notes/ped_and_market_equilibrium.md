# Economics Topic Note — PED and Market Equilibrium

## Market equilibrium
Equilibrium is the point where quantity demanded equals quantity supplied. At equilibrium, there is no shortage or surplus, so there is no pressure for price to change.

## Excess demand and excess supply
If quantity demanded is greater than quantity supplied, there is excess demand, so price tends to rise. If quantity supplied is greater than quantity demanded, there is excess supply, so price tends to fall.

## Price elasticity of demand (PED)
PED measures how responsive quantity demanded is to a change in price.
- **Elastic demand**: quantity demanded changes a lot.
- **Inelastic demand**: quantity demanded changes a little.

## What affects PED
PED depends on:
- availability of substitutes
- whether the good is a necessity or luxury
- proportion of income spent on the good
- time available to adjust

## Why PED matters
PED is important for business pricing decisions and for government tax policy. If demand is inelastic, a tax may raise significant revenue because quantity demanded does not fall very much.

## Consumer and producer surplus
At equilibrium, both consumers and producers gain surplus. Government intervention such as taxes and subsidies can change these surpluses.

## Common exam ideas
- Define PED.
- Explain why a product may have elastic or inelastic demand.
- Analyse the effect of price change depending on PED.
- Explain equilibrium using a diagram.
