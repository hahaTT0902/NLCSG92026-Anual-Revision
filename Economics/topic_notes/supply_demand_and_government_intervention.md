# Economics Topic Note — Supply, Demand and Government Intervention

## Demand
Demand refers to the quantity of a good or service consumers are willing and able to buy at different prices. The law of demand states that when price falls, quantity demanded rises, ceteris paribus.

## Supply
Supply refers to the quantity of a good or service producers are willing and able to sell at different prices. The law of supply states that when price rises, quantity supplied rises, ceteris paribus.

## Equilibrium
Equilibrium occurs where demand equals supply. At this price, there is no tendency for price to change. If there is excess demand, price tends to rise. If there is excess supply, price tends to fall.

## Consumer and producer surplus
Consumer surplus is the difference between what consumers are willing to pay and what they actually pay. Producer surplus is the difference between the market price and the minimum producers are willing to accept.

## Indirect taxes
An indirect tax increases production costs and shifts the supply curve to the left. This usually raises the market price and reduces the quantity traded. The government gains tax revenue.

## Subsidies
A subsidy lowers production costs and shifts supply to the right. This usually lowers price and increases quantity traded. Governments use subsidies to encourage socially beneficial goods.
