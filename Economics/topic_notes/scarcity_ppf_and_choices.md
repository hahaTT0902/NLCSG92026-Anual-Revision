# Economics Topic Note — Scarcity, PPF and Choices

## Scarcity
Scarcity exists because resources are limited but human wants are unlimited. This means people, firms and governments must make choices.

## Opportunity cost
Whenever a choice is made, the next best alternative is given up. This is called opportunity cost. Opportunity cost helps economists evaluate decisions.

## Factors of production
Resources used to produce goods and services are called factors of production:
- **Land**: natural resources
- **Labour**: human effort
- **Capital**: man-made aids to production
- **Entrepreneurship**: organising resources and taking risks

## Circular flow of income
Households provide factors of production to firms and receive income in return. Firms sell goods and services to households. This model helps explain how money and resources move around an economy.

## Production Possibility Frontier (PPF)
A PPF shows the maximum combinations of two goods an economy can produce with available resources and technology.
- Points on the curve = efficient
- Points inside = inefficient / unemployed resources
- Points outside = unattainable with current resources

## Why the PPF is useful
A PPF illustrates scarcity, choice, opportunity cost and economic growth. If the curve shifts outward, it shows growth caused by more resources or better productivity.

## Common exam ideas
- Define scarcity and opportunity cost.
- Explain why a PPF slopes downward.
- Distinguish efficiency and inefficiency.
- Explain how growth shifts a PPF outward.
