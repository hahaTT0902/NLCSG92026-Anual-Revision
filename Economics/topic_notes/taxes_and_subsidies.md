# Economics Topic Note — Taxes and Subsidies

## Indirect tax
An indirect tax is imposed on spending on goods and services. It raises production costs and shifts supply to the left.

## Effects of indirect tax
Price usually rises, quantity traded usually falls, and the government gains revenue. Taxes are often used to discourage demerit goods.

## Subsidy
A subsidy is a payment from the government to producers. It lowers costs of production and shifts supply to the right.

## Effects of subsidy
Price usually falls, quantity traded usually rises, and the government spends money. Subsidies are often used to encourage merit goods.

## Stakeholder effects
Consumers, producers and government are all affected differently by tax and subsidy policies.
