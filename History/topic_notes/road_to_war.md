# History Topic Note — The Road to War

## Long-term causes
The Treaty of Versailles left Germany resentful. The League of Nations was weak. The Great Depression increased instability. Appeasement encouraged Hitler’s aggression.

## Appeasement
Britain and France followed appeasement partly because they feared another major war and were not fully prepared for conflict. They also believed some German complaints were understandable.

## Key steps
- Rearmament
- Rhineland
- Anschluss
- Munich Agreement
- Nazi-Soviet Pact
- Invasion of Poland

## Why war broke out
War broke out because diplomacy failed to stop aggressive expansion. By 1939, trust had collapsed and Hitler was willing to use force.

## Exam focus
- Explain long-term and short-term causes.
- Evaluate how far appeasement was responsible.
