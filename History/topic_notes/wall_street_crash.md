# History Topic Note — The Wall Street Crash

## Background
During the 1920s, the USA appeared prosperous. However, there were serious weaknesses beneath the surface, including overproduction, uneven wealth distribution, speculation and weak banking structures.

## What happened?
The Wall Street Crash of 1929 was a dramatic collapse in share prices. Investors lost confidence and rushed to sell shares. This helped trigger a wider economic crisis.

## Why it was significant
The crash mattered because it destroyed confidence in the economy. Businesses failed, banks collapsed and unemployment rose sharply. It also exposed the fragility of an economy built on credit, speculation and unequal wealth.

## Social impact
Many Americans lost jobs, savings and homes. Shanty towns appeared, and people queued for food. Fear and insecurity increased across society.

## Was the crash the only cause?
No. The crash was a major turning point, but underlying weaknesses already existed. It was a catalyst that turned existing economic weaknesses into a full depression.

## Exam focus
- Why was the crash significant?
- To what extent was it the main cause of the Great Depression?
- Which groups were most affected and why?
