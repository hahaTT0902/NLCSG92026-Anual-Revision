# History — Exam Tips

1. Answer the exact question, not the topic in general.
2. Use specific evidence: dates, people, events, laws, agreements.
3. Build paragraphs with PEEL or similar structure.
4. Explain why evidence is significant.
5. In causation questions, compare factors instead of listing them.
6. In significance questions, explain impact and importance.
7. In judgement questions, make your argument clear from the start.
8. Balance different sides of the argument.
9. Avoid vague words like ‘things got worse’ — explain how and for whom.
10. End with a conclusion that directly answers the question.
