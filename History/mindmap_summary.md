# Grade 9 History — Mind Map Summary

## 1. Unit 1 — The significance of the Wall Street Crash
### Historical context
- The US in the 1920s experienced prosperity, but growth was uneven.
- Credit and consumer spending increased rapidly.
- Confidence in banks and speculation helped create the boom.

### Why the crash was significant
- The Wall Street Crash shook confidence in the economy.
- Businesses collapsed and unemployment rose.
- Farmers, workers and poorer groups suffered badly.
- Social impact included homelessness, fear and migration in search of work.
- Political pressure for government action increased.

### How far was the crash the main cause of how the USA was affected?
- The crash was a major catalyst.
- But weaknesses already existed: overproduction, weak banks, debt and unequal wealth.
- The crash exposed and deepened these problems.

## 2. Unit 2 — The Road to War, 1919 to 1941
### Long-term causes of a second world war
- The Treaty of Versailles left Germany dissatisfied.
- The League of Nations was weak.
- Appeasement encouraged aggression.
- Bias against communism shaped decisions.

### Why appeasement mattered
- Britain and France made concessions to avoid war.
- The Munich Agreement encouraged Hitler.
- It showed that strong resistance was lacking.

### Final steps to war
- Nazi-Soviet Pact removed a major obstacle for Hitler.
- Germany invaded Poland in September 1939.
- Britain and France declared war.
- The USA joined later after the attack on Pearl Harbor in 1941.

## 3. Unit 3 — The Civil Rights Movement in the USA
### Judgement to the movement
- Change was significant but incomplete.
- Both federal action and activism mattered.
- African Americans still faced discrimination even after legal changes.

### Campaigns and methods in the 1950s and 1960s
- Activists used non-violent protest, legal challenges and marches.
- Key examples include Brown v. Board, Montgomery, sit-ins, Freedom Rides and Birmingham.
- Leadership and grassroots organisation both mattered.

### To what extent did stronger identity cause success?
- Pride, organisation and shared goals helped mobilisation.
- But success also depended on media attention, federal government action and wider politics.

## 4. Unit 6 — Peace and Conflict in the Middle East
### Historical roots of conflict
- Ottoman decline, British involvement and Zionism shaped the region.
- Borders, mandates and broken promises created long-term tensions.
- The creation of Israel in 1948 intensified conflict.

### Why peace has been difficult
- Territorial disputes, refugees, Jerusalem, settlements and security fears remain unresolved.
- Regional and international involvement complicates peace.
- Violence deepens mistrust.

### Can a militarised peace be achieved?
- Military force may bring temporary stability.
- But long-term peace usually requires compromise, diplomacy and mutual recognition.
- The map suggests both military and diplomatic limits should be considered.
