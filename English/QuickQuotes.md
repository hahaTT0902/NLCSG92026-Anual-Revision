## Violence
1. **Embedded in Papa**  
   a. “Papa said we were to recite 16 different novenas for Mama’s forgiveness.”  
   b. “He poured the water in bowl and soaked my hand in it.”  

2. **Silences its victims and represses their thoughts**  
   a. “I bit my lips so that my mouth would not betray me.”  
   b. “Papa liked order.”  
   c. “Imagine what *The Standard* would be if we never spoke up.”  

3. **Distorts love and turns family into a place of fear**  
   a. “I knew that when the tea burned my tongue, it burned Papa’s love into me.”

## Silence
1. **A tool of control**   
   a. “Papa liked order.”  
   b. “I wanted to say I’m sorry.”  
   c. “They should not have written that.”  

2. **Broken by love, freedom, and joy**  
   a. “I bit my lips so that my mouth would not betray me.”  
   b. “That night, I was dreaming that I was laughing, but it did not sound like my laughter.”  
   c. “I felt that I was at home, that I was where I had been meant to be.”  

3. **Silence can be broken**  
   a. “I laughed… I was not sure I had ever heard myself laugh.”  
   b. “As he drove, we sang Igbo choruses.”

## Religion
1. **Used as a tool of control**  
   a. “Papa said we were to recite 16 different novenas for Mama’s forgiveness.”  
   b. “Papa had offered to build him a house, buy him a car, and hire him a driver as long as he converted and threw away the chi in the shrine.”  
   c. “He was not Catholic, that was all. He was not of the faith.”  

2. **Distorted by Papa, but humanised through Father Amadi and Aunty Ifeoma**  
   a. “We do not say mass in the name of grace like your father does.”  
   b. “Papa-Nnukwu was not a heathen but a traditionalist; sometimes what was different was just as good as what was familiar.”  
   c. “Father Amadi included Jaja and me in the conversation, asking us questions. Yet I remained silent.”  

3. **Moves from fear to liberation**  
   a. “I pressed my lips together, biting my lower lip, so my mouth would not betray me.”  
   b. “I was always a penitent when I was close to a priest at confession, but it was hard to feel penitent now.”  
   c. “As he drove, we sang Igbo choruses.”

## Family
1. **Papa’s family is built on control and fear**  
   a. “Papa liked order.”  
   b. “Don’t touch any food, don’t drink anything. And as usual, you will stay for not longer than fifteen minutes.”  
   c. “We did not scale the rod because we thought we could, we scaled it because we were terrified that we couldn’t.”  

2. **Love in Papa’s family is distorted by violence**  
   a. “I knew that when the tea burned my tongue, it burned Papa’s love into me.”  
   b. “Papa said we were to recite 16 different novenas for Mama’s forgiveness.”  
   c. “There is so much more that Mama and I do not talk about.”  

3. **Aunty Ifeoma’s family offers warmth, freedom, and belonging**  
   a. “Laughter always rang out in Aunty Ifeoma’s house.”  
   b. “I felt that I was at home, that I was where I had been meant to be.”  
   c. “I laughed. It sounded strange… I was not sure I had ever heard myself laugh.”
