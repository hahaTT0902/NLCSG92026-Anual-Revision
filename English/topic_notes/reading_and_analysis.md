# English Topic Note — Reading and Analysis

## Understanding the question
Always identify what the question is asking. Look for command words such as explain, analyse, compare, evaluate or explore.

## Finding evidence
Choose short, precise quotations. Avoid copying large chunks of text. The best quotations contain words you can analyse closely.

## Analysing language
Writers use devices such as metaphor, simile, personification, repetition, contrast and imagery. Do not stop at naming the technique; explain its effect.

## Analysing structure
Structure refers to how the text is organised. Look at openings, endings, shifts in focus, changes in pace, paragraph order and repetition.

## Writer’s viewpoint and tone
Ask how the writer feels and how the reader is influenced. Tone could be angry, hopeful, sarcastic, formal, reflective or fearful.

## Building paragraphs
Use PEEL or PEE:
- Point
- Evidence
- Explanation
- Link
Each paragraph should answer part of the question clearly.

## Common exam advice
- Zoom in on single words.
- Explain connotations and associations.
- Link your analysis to the question every time.
- Compare similarities and differences carefully when asked.
