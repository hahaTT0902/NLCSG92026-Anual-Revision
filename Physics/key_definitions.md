# Physics — Key Definitions

- **Force**: a push or pull that can change motion.
- **Vector**: quantity with magnitude and direction.
- **Acceleration**: rate of change of velocity.
- **Momentum**: mass × velocity.
- **Impulse**: force × time = change in momentum.
- **Work done**: energy transferred when a force moves an object through a distance.
- **Power**: rate of energy transfer or work done.
- **Efficiency**: useful output divided by total input.
- **Transverse wave**: wave in which vibrations are perpendicular to the direction of travel.
- **Longitudinal wave**: wave in which vibrations are parallel to the direction of travel.
- **Refraction**: change in direction of a wave due to a change in speed.
- **Refractive index**: measure of how much a material slows down light.
