# Physics Topic Note — Energy and Conservation

## Energy stores
Useful energy stores include kinetic, gravitational potential, elastic potential, chemical, thermal and nuclear. Energy can be transferred from one store to another.

## Conservation of energy
Energy cannot be created or destroyed. It can only be transferred or transformed. In real systems, some energy is dissipated to less useful stores, often as heat.

## Kinetic energy
Kinetic energy is the energy of motion. Faster objects and more massive objects have more kinetic energy.

## Gravitational potential energy
GPE is energy stored because of height in a gravitational field. The higher an object is raised, the more GPE it gains.

## Elastic potential energy
Elastic potential energy is stored when an elastic object such as a spring is stretched or compressed. Hooke’s Law states that extension is proportional to force up to the limit of proportionality.

## Work done
Work done is the energy transferred when a force moves an object through a distance in the direction of the force.

## Power
Power is the rate at which work is done or energy is transferred. A more powerful machine transfers energy faster.

## Efficiency
Efficiency compares useful output with total input. No machine is 100% efficient in practice because some energy is dissipated.

## Common exam ideas
- Identify stores and transfer pathways.
- Explain why total energy is conserved.
- Calculate work done, power and efficiency.
- Explain why some energy becomes less useful.
