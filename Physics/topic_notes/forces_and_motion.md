# Physics Topic Note — Forces and Motion

## Force as a vector
Force has both size and direction. When several forces act on an object, they combine to give a resultant force.

## Resultant force
If forces are balanced, the resultant force is zero and motion does not change. If forces are unbalanced, the object accelerates.

## Speed and motion
Speed tells us how fast an object moves. Motion can be described using speed, direction and graphs.

## Friction and resistive forces
Friction opposes motion between surfaces. Air resistance and water resistance are resistive forces that oppose motion through fluids.

## Mass and weight
Mass is the amount of matter in an object. Weight is the force caused by gravity acting on that mass.

## Exam focus
- Calculate resultant force.
- Explain balanced and unbalanced forces.
- Distinguish mass from weight.
