# Physics Topic Note — Waves and Refraction

## What waves do
Waves transfer energy from one place to another without net transfer of matter. They can be mechanical or electromagnetic.

## Transverse and longitudinal waves
- **Transverse waves** vibrate at right angles to the direction of travel.
- **Longitudinal waves** vibrate parallel to the direction of travel.
Light is transverse; sound is longitudinal.

## Wave properties
Important wave properties are wavelength, frequency, amplitude and period.
- **Frequency** = number of waves per second.
- **Period** = time for one wave.
- **Wave speed** = frequency × wavelength.

## Reflection
When waves reflect, the angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection. This applies to light and many other waves.

## Refraction
Refraction happens when a wave enters a different medium and changes speed. For light, this often causes a change in direction. If light slows down, it bends towards the normal; if it speeds up, it bends away from the normal.

## Refractive index
Refractive index is a measure of how much a medium slows down light. A higher refractive index means light travels more slowly in that material.

## Electromagnetic spectrum
The EM spectrum includes radio, microwaves, infrared, visible light, ultraviolet, X-rays and gamma rays. They differ in frequency and wavelength but all travel at the same speed in a vacuum.

## Common exam ideas
- Distinguish transverse and longitudinal waves.
- Use the wave speed equation.
- Draw and explain reflection/refraction ray diagrams.
- Describe uses and hazards of EM waves.
