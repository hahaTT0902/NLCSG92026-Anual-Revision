# Physics Topic Note — Newton’s Laws and Momentum

## Newton’s First Law
An object stays at rest, or continues moving at constant velocity, unless acted on by a resultant force. This law explains inertia.

## Newton’s Second Law
The acceleration of an object depends on the resultant force and its mass. The relationship is given by F = ma. A larger force causes greater acceleration; a larger mass causes smaller acceleration for the same force.

## Newton’s Third Law
When two objects interact, they exert equal and opposite forces on each other. These forces act on different objects, so they do not cancel each other out.

## Momentum
Momentum is the product of mass and velocity. It is a vector quantity, so direction matters.

## Conservation of momentum
In a closed system, total momentum before an interaction equals total momentum after the interaction. This is useful in collisions and explosions.

## Impulse
Impulse is the product of force and time. It equals the change in momentum. Increasing the time of impact reduces the force, which explains airbags and crumple zones.

## Exam focus
- State all three laws accurately.
- Use F = ma in calculations.
- Use momentum conservation in collision problems.
- Explain why force pairs in Newton’s Third Law do not cancel.
