# Grade 9 Physics — Mind Map Summary

## 1. Unit 1 — Force and Newton’s Three Laws
### Force and vectors
- Force is an interaction that can change motion.
- Force is a vector quantity with magnitude and direction.
- Represent forces using arrows.
- Forces can cause acceleration, deceleration or change of direction.

### Newton’s First Law
- Objects stay at rest or move at constant velocity unless acted on by a resultant force.
- Links to inertia.

### Newton’s Second Law
- Resultant force causes acceleration.
- F = ma.
- Acceleration has the same direction as the resultant force.

### Newton’s Third Law
- When two objects interact, they exert equal and opposite forces on each other.
- Action and reaction forces act on different objects.

### Momentum and conservation
- Momentum = mass × velocity.
- Momentum is a vector quantity.
- Total momentum is conserved in an isolated system.

### Collisions and impulse
- Elastic and inelastic collisions behave differently.
- Impulse equals force × time and equals change in momentum.

## 2. Unit 2 — Energy and Conservation of Energy
### Energy fundamentals
- Energy is the ability to cause change or do work.
- The SI unit of energy is the joule.

### Energy stores and transfers
- Examples: kinetic, gravitational potential, chemical, thermal, elastic.
- Energy can be transferred mechanically, electrically, by heating or by radiation.

### Principle of conservation of energy
- Energy cannot be created or destroyed.
- It changes form and may become less useful.

### Kinetic energy and gravitational potential energy
- Kinetic energy depends on mass and speed.
- GPE depends on mass, gravitational field strength and height.
- Apply equations to simple situations.

### Elastic potential energy and spring constant
- Springs store elastic energy.
- Understand spring constant and Hooke’s Law ideas where relevant.

### Work, power and efficiency
- Work done = force × distance in the direction of movement.
- Power is the rate of energy transfer.
- Efficiency = useful output ÷ total input.

## 3. Unit 3 — Waves Phenomenon
### Basic wave ideas and types
- Waves transfer energy without transferring matter overall.
- Distinguish between transverse and longitudinal waves.
- Wave features include crest, trough, compression and rarefaction.

### Wave properties and relationships
- Know amplitude, wavelength, frequency and period.
- Frequency = 1 / period.
- Wave speed = frequency × wavelength.

### Light, sound and wavefronts
- Light is transverse; sound is longitudinal.
- Wavefronts help represent wave direction.
- Reflection and refraction are key ideas.

### Reflection of waves
- Angle of incidence = angle of reflection.
- Draw ray diagrams.
- Explain reflection from different surfaces.

### Refraction and refractive index
- Refraction happens when a wave changes speed in a new medium.
- Light bends towards the normal when it slows down.
- Refractive index measures how much a medium slows light.
- Use refractive index equations where required.

### Electromagnetic spectrum
- EM waves differ in wavelength and frequency.
- Learn uses and hazards of different parts of the spectrum.
