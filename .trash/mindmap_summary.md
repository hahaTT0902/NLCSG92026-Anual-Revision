## 2. Unit 1 — The Economist
### Factors of production
- Land = natural resources
- Labour = human effort
- Capital = man-made resources
- Entrepreneurship = organising and risk-taking

### Factor income
- Wages = labour
- Rent = land
- Interest = capital
- Profit = entrepreneurs

### Circular flow of income model
- Households and firms interact.
- Households provide factors and receive income.
- Firms produce goods/services which households buy.
- Money flow and resource flow continue.

### Opportunity cost and the basic economic problem
- Scarcity means limited resources but unlimited wants.
- Choices must be made.
- Opportunity cost is the next best alternative given up.

### Production possibility frontier (PPF)
- Shows maximum output combinations of two goods.
- Points on the curve are efficient.
- Points inside are inefficient.
- Points outside are unattainable.
- PPF shows opportunity cost, growth and unemployment.

## 3. Unit 2 — The Market Analyst
### Law of demand
- If price falls, quantity demanded rises.
- If price rises, quantity demanded falls.
- Ceteris paribus means other factors stay constant.

### Law of supply
- If price rises, quantity supplied rises.
- If price falls, quantity supplied falls.
- Also assumes ceteris paribus.

### Price mechanism and equilibrium
- Markets allocate resources through price changes.
- Equilibrium occurs when demand equals supply.
- Excess demand causes price to rise.
- Excess supply causes price to fall.

### Allocative efficiency
- Occurs at equilibrium.
- Maximises society’s welfare.
- Consumer surplus = willingness to pay minus actual price.
- Producer surplus = market price minus minimum acceptable price.

### Price elasticity of demand (PED)
- Measures responsiveness of quantity demanded to price.
- Elastic = changes a lot.
- Inelastic = changes a little.
- Important for businesses and government policy.

## 4. Unit 3 — The Policy Maker
### Government intervention
#### Indirect taxes
- Imposed on goods/services.
- Increases firms’ costs and shifts supply left.
- Raises price and reduces quantity traded.
- Government receives tax revenue.
- Reduces demerit goods.

#### Subsidies
- Payment from government to producers.
- Lowers production costs and shifts supply right.
- Lowers price and increases quantity traded.
- Encourages merit goods.

### Analysis and diagrams
- Be able to explain tax diagrams and subsidy diagrams.
- Explain effects on price, quantity, consumers, producers and government.
